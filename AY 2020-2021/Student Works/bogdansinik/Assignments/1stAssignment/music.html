Rock & Roll

Rock and roll (often written as rock & roll, rock 'n' roll, or rock 'n roll) is a genre of popular music that evolved in the United States during the late
1940s and early 1950s.It originated from musical styles such as gospel, jump blues, jazz, boogie woogie, rhythm and blues, and country music.
While rock and roll's formative elements can be heard in blues records from the 1920s, and in country records of the 1930s, the genre did not acquire
its name until 1954.n the earliest rock and roll styles, either the piano or saxophone was typically the lead instrument. These instruments were generally
replaced or supplemented by guitar in the middle to late 1950s.The beat is essentially a dance rhythm with an accentuated backbeat, almost always provided
by a snare drum. Classic rock and roll is usually played with one or two electric guitars (one lead, one rhythm) and a double bass (string bass). After the mid-1950s,
electric bass guitars and drum kits became popular in classic rock.
